Overview

What the variant is and why it's being analyzed

FieldValue
GeneBCL2
Mutation (CDS)
Mutation (AA)p.P59S

The BCL2 p.P59S variant lies in the flexible loop domain and has been observed in lymphomas [6, 7]. This region contains known regulatory phosphosites, including Thr56/Ser70/Ser87 [3, 4, 5]. Within the analyzed 29-mer, Thr56 is evidence-supported in EPSD and is adjacent to the P59 residue [9]. Our analysis indicates that P59S creates a new serine-centered phosphosite candidate at position 59 near Thr56, with both sites solvent-accessible in structural context.

Hypotheses

Mechanistic explanations generated from the overview and literature.

BCL2 p.P59S creates a novel ERN1/IRE1-licensed phospho-switch at Ser59 that strengthens BCL2 pro-survival signaling in DLBCL by coupling UPR stress to mitochondrial apoptosis control

The BCL2 p.P59S substitution in the flexible loop domain introduces a new solvent-accessible Ser at center_pos 59 with a predicted ERN1/IRE1 specificity gain. This could couple ER stress/UPR signaling to BCL2’s anti-apoptotic control in lymphoma cells (direction of S59 phosphorylation to be measured) [1, 6, 7].

Disease context

Predicted observations

  • Detect BCL2 Ser59 phosphorylation specifically in P59S cells by targeted LC–MS/MS; modulation with ER stress and ERN1 perturbation.
  • ERN1 perturbation reduces S59 phospho-signal and sensitizes P59S cells to apoptosis under ER stress versus WT; site epistasis with S59A/D/E.
Laboratory validation

Suggested models

  • DLBCL cell lines: WT vs P59S isogenic

Assays

  • Targeted phosphoproteomics for S59
  • ERN1 inhibition/knockdown + apoptosis readouts
  • Epistasis: S59A/S59D(E)

Controls and confounders

  • Match BCL2 levels/localization
  • Verify UPR (XBP1 splicing)
  • Monitor S70 as comparator

Feasibility notes

MS-based detection feasible without pS59 antibody

Limitations
  • ERN1→BCL2 S59 is predicted; requires direct phospho evidence.
  • IRE1 access to BCL2 FLD must be demonstrated in cells.
  • FLD has multiple PTM sites; combinatorial effects are possible.
