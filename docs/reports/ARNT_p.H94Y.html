Overview

What the variant is and why it's being analyzed

FieldValue
GeneARNT
Mutation (CDS)
Mutation (AA)p.H94Y

ARNT p.H94Y (HIS94TYR; aa_mut_start=94, aa_mut_stop=94) lies in the N-terminal region adjacent to a known phosphoserine at S95 [1, 5]. Within the 29-mer window, the variant creates a new central Y94 phospho-acceptor window and retains an adjacent evidence-supported S95 window; both are solvent-accessible by RSA ≥0.1 [1, 5]. Kinase-specificity analysis predicts substantial gains in tyrosine kinase recognition at the Y94-centered window (e.g., EGFR/ERBB2, FGFR1/2, DDR1, EPH family, FAK/PYK2; 86–96 percentile), meeting significance thresholds [8]. Given ARNT’s role as HIF-1β and as an AhR partner, phosphorylation changes near the N-terminus could impact dimerization or DNA-binding dynamics in HIF/AhR signaling [2, 3].

Hypotheses

Mechanistic explanations generated from the overview and literature.

ARNT p.H94Y creates a novel Y94 RTK/FAK docking-phosphosite that couples EGFR/HER2/FGFR2/FAK signaling to HIF/AhR transcriptional output in clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC)

ARNT (HIF-1β) is an obligate heterodimer partner for HIF-α and also partners with AhR, enabling HIF/AhR transcriptional programs [1, 3]. The ARNT p.H94Y substitution introduces a new solvent-accessible phospho-acceptor at Y94 (center_pos=94) within the N-terminal bHLH-PAS region [2], with predicted kinase-substrate specificity gains for EGFR, HER2, FGFR2, and FAK at this Y94-centered motif (all direction=gain), implying rewired recognition rather than proven phosphorylation [1]. If Y94 becomes phosphorylated in cells, it is predicted to create a conditional, growth-factor/adhesion–responsive control point on ARNT that modulates ARNT’s transcriptional complex behavior, thereby altering HIF/AhR output in RCC models where HIF–AHR interplay is disease-relevant [3, 4]. This provides a mechanistic route for RTK/FAK signaling to influence HIF activity in cancer, consistent with prior evidence that HER2→Akt signaling can engage HIF-1β (ARNT) to regulate HIF transcriptional activity [9].

Predicted observations

  • Phospho-readout: targeted MS (PRM/SRM) detects a Y94-phosphopeptide in ARNT p.H94Y but not ARNT-WT, and its abundance changes upon acute EGF, heregulin, or FGF stimulation and/or FAK pathway perturbation (direction to be empirically determined) [1].
  • Kinase-dependency/discrimination: pharmacologic inhibition or CRISPR knockdown of EGFR vs ERBB2 vs FGFR2 vs PTK2 (FAK) differentially reduces the Y94 phospho-signal and separates which kinase axis is primarily responsible for Y94 modification in the relevant model (discriminating readout) [9].
  • Transcriptional consequence: ARNT p.H94Y cells show altered HIF and/or AhR transcriptional reporter activity (e.g., HRE-luc and XRE/DRE-luc) in response to growth factor or adhesion cues compared with ARNT-WT, consistent with Y94 acting as a conditional control point (direction to be empirically determined) [1, 3].
  • Complex behavior: ARNT p.H94Y shows altered stimulus-dependent interaction dynamics with HIF-α (HIF1A or EPAS1) and/or AhR in co-IP/BioID/AP-MS relative to WT, consistent with phospho-dependent docking/repulsion at the N-terminus (direction to be empirically determined) [1, 2].
Laboratory validation

Suggested models

  • ccRCC cell lines with robust HIF signaling (e.g., VHL-defective models) engineered to express ARNT-WT vs ARNT-H94Y at comparable levels [4]
  • Kidney epithelial models with inducible HIF/AhR reporter constructs to isolate ARNT-dependent transcriptional outputs [1, 3]

Assays

  • Targeted phosphoproteomics (PRM/SRM) for ARNT pY94 and nearby site coverage (including the adjacent S95 region) under ±EGF/FGF stimulation and ±FAK activation conditions [5]
  • Kinase perturbations: EGFR inhibitor, ERBB2 inhibitor, FGFR inhibitor, and FAK inhibitor or PTK2 CRISPRi/siRNA, followed by pY94 readout and HRE/XRE reporter assays [9]
  • Site perturbation/epistasis: generate ARNT-H94Y/Y94F (non-phosphorylatable) and ARNT-H94Y/Y94E (phosphomimetic proxy) alleles; test whether Y94F abolishes kinase-perturbation sensitivity of the transcriptional phenotype (rescue/epistasis) [1, 2]
  • Protein interaction mapping (co-IP or proximity labeling) for ARNT–HIF-α and ARNT–AhR complexes ±kinase inhibitors to test whether Y94-dependent phosphorylation state controls complex assembly [1, 3]

Controls and confounders

  • Control for ARNT abundance and nuclear localization (rule out expression/localization artifacts) [1]
  • Include ARNT-WT plus empty-vector controls and matched stimulation time courses to distinguish basal vs inducible effects [9]
  • Verify that any inhibitor effects on reporters are not due to global toxicity or off-target kinase suppression (use multiple inhibitors or genetic perturbations per kinase) [9]

Feasibility notes

Because Y94 is a mutation-created site, phospho-specific antibodies may not exist; targeted MS is the most direct initial readout, followed by custom antibody development if the site is robustly detected [5].

Limitations
  • Kinase_events represent predicted substrate specificity rewiring at Y94, not confirmed phosphorylation; the existence, stoichiometry, and directionality of pY94 in vivo must be established experimentally [1].
  • The specific downstream effect of Y94 phosphorylation on ARNT complex assembly/function is not yet defined; multiple outcomes (enhanced or impaired HIF/AhR transcription) remain plausible until tested with site mutants [2, 3].
  • Disease relevance is grounded at the pathway level (HIF/AhR in RCC) rather than mutation-specific clinical evidence for ARNT p.H94Y, which may limit translational generality [4].
