Overview

What the variant is and why it's being analyzed

Mutation (CDS)
Mutation (AA)p.P694S

EGFR p.P694S (PRO694SER) occurs within the proximal cytoplasmic region adjacent to the well-characterized Thr693 phosphosite on EGFR [3]. The variant has been reported in cancer cohorts and case reports, including non-small cell lung cancer, and cataloged by OncoKB as a somatic EGFR mutation observed in melanoma and NSCLC [1, 2]. The local T693 site is a documented phosphorylation position, indicating this region’s relevance to phosphorylation-dependent regulation [3].

Hypotheses

Mechanistic explanations generated from the overview and literature.

EGFR p.P694S rewires proximal juxtamembrane phosphorylation (loss at T693; gain at de novo S694) to couple EGFR trafficking priming with NF-κB signaling in NSCLC

EGFR p.P694S is adjacent to the known phosphosite EGFR T693 (center_pos=693) and creates a new phospho-acceptor at S694 (center_pos=694), producing two local specificity rewiring signals [3]. At T693, the mutation context shows a broad predicted loss of kinase specificity for MAPKs including MAPK14/p38 (direction=loss) and MAPK1/3/ERK (direction=loss), which may reduce the capacity for RTK cross-talk ‘priming’ at recycling endosomes that has been shown to depend on EGFR T693 phosphorylation [6]. In parallel, the de novo S694 site shows predicted gain of IKK complex kinase specificity (CHUK/IKKα and IKBKB/IKKβ; direction=gain), potentially enabling a new linkage between EGFR juxtamembrane phosphorylation and NF-κB pathway engagement in tumor cells [3]. This combined shift is proposed to favor an EGFR signaling state that mixes altered receptor trafficking/priming with NF-κB–associated pro-survival or inflammatory transcriptional programs relevant to EGFR-driven cancers including NSCLC where this mutation has been observed [1, 2, 4, 5].

Predicted observations

  • Site-level phospho readouts: compared with EGFR-WT, EGFR-P694S cells will show altered phosphorylation at EGFR T693 and/or detectable phosphorylation at de novo EGFR S694; directionality should be established by targeted phosphoproteomics (PRM/SRM) covering both peptides and, if available, phospho-specific antibodies for pT693 [3].
  • Kinase-dependency shift: pharmacologic or genetic inhibition of IKBKB/CHUK will preferentially reduce NF-κB pathway activity (e.g., p-IκBα levels and NF-κB reporter) in EGFR-P694S versus EGFR-WT backgrounds, consistent with S694 gaining IKK-family recognition potential [3].
  • Trafficking/priming phenotype: EGFR-P694S will show altered EGFR recycling/endosomal residence time after ligand stimulation and/or altered FGFR→EGFR priming behavior compared with EGFR-WT, consistent with T693 being a trafficking/priming node and predicted MAPK-family loss of recognition at T693 [6].
  • Mechanism-discriminating epistasis: introducing an EGFR S694A mutation in the P694S background (P694S+S694A) will selectively blunt NF-κB readouts without restoring T693-linked trafficking changes, whereas introducing EGFR T693A in an otherwise WT background will phenocopy the trafficking/priming defect but not the IKK-dependency, separating the two arms [3, 6].
Laboratory validation

Suggested models

  • Isogenic EGFR-WT vs EGFR-P694S knock-in NSCLC cell line (e.g., PC9 or HCC827 derivative) [4, 5]
  • HEK293 or Ba/F3 engineered expression system for clean signaling/trafficking dissection [4]

Assays

  • Targeted LC–MS/MS phosphoproteomics for EGFR pT693 and pS694 peptides (±EGF stimulation; ±endocytosis/recycling perturbations) [3, 6]
  • NF-κB pathway activity: NF-κB luciferase reporter and immunoblots for p-IκBα / p-p65 (±IKBKB/CHUK inhibition or knockout) [3]
  • EGFR trafficking: surface biotinylation recycling assay and confocal co-localization with recycling endosome markers following EGF stimulation [6]
  • Phenotype: proliferation/survival under EGFR TKI treatment ± IKK inhibition to test adaptive signaling coupling in an EGFR-oncogene context [4, 5]

Controls and confounders

  • Phosphosite causality controls: EGFR-P694S+S694A and EGFR-P694S+S694D/E (phosphomimetic proxy) alongside EGFR-WT and EGFR-T693A [3].
  • Kinase perturbation specificity controls: use at least two orthogonal IKBKB/CHUK perturbations (small molecule + CRISPR/siRNA) and verify pathway engagement via canonical NF-κB markers to avoid off-target interpretations [3].
  • Separate ‘specificity rewiring’ from ‘phosphorylation level’: quantify kinase activation state (e.g., p38/ERK activity markers) alongside site phosphorylation to distinguish altered upstream kinase activity from altered motif recognition [4].

Feasibility notes

If no commercial pS694 antibody exists, PRM/SRM targeted MS is the primary readout for de novo S694 phosphorylation and can be paired with existing EGFR pT693 assays or MS transitions [3].

Limitations
  • Kinase events represent predicted motif-recognition changes, not confirmed phosphorylation changes; both pT693 and pS694 occupancy must be experimentally established before inferring signaling consequences [3].
  • Direct evidence that IKK phosphorylates EGFR at S694 is not yet established; this is a testable prediction derived from specificity gain and requires in vitro kinase assays or cellular dependency tests [3].
  • The strongest trafficking/priming literature support is for T693 as a regulatory node, but the exact upstream kinase(s) responsible in the relevant cancer context may differ from the predicted MAPK-family set and must be empirically mapped [4, 6].

Interpretation
Context
  • Disease: lung adenocarcinoma (NSCLC subtype) vs normal
  • Tissue: lung
  • Primary cell type: pulmonary alveolar type 2 (AT2) cells (n_disease=12,635; n_normal=278,182)
  • Genes tested: EGFR, IKBKB, CHUK, MAPK14, MAPK1, MAPK3
Mechanistic expectations
  • Hypothesis: EGFR p.P694S rewires juxtamembrane phosphorylation (T693 loss/S694 gain) to couple EGFR trafficking priming with NF-κB signaling in NSCLC
  • This is a post-translational rewiring; no explicit mRNA up/down is required, though NF-κB pathway activation could occur without kinase mRNA increases
Observed results (AT2; disease vs normal)
  • Down in disease with non-trivial magnitude and q≤0.05: EGFR (logFC=-0.41, q=0.024), MAPK1 (ERK2; -0.26, q=0.024), MAPK14 (p38α; -0.14, q=0.024), IKBKB (IKKβ; -0.11, q=0.024), CHUK (IKKα; -0.03, q=0.048)
  • Near-null: MAPK3 (ERK1; -0.01, q=0.129)
Cross-check (aggregated epithelial cell of lung; disease vs normal)
  • Weak/inconclusive shifts: EGFR (+0.08, q=0.105), MAPK3 (-0.11, q=0.105); others near-zero with q>0.10
Alignment assessment
  • The AT2-specific analysis shows coordinated downregulation of EGFR and core kinases (IKKβ/IKKα, ERK2, p38α), which does not provide positive evidence for heightened NF-κB signaling at the mRNA level
  • However, because the hypothesis centers on phosphorylation/trafficking rewiring, mRNA decreases do not necessarily contradict kinase activity; scRNA cannot read out NF-κB activation state
  • The epithelial-aggregated slice is broadly uninformative (borderline q-values, small magnitudes)
Conclusion
  • Verdict: UNINFORMATIVE
  • Rationale: PTM-centric mechanism lacks required mRNA-direction prediction; despite coordinated mRNA decreases in malignant AT2 cells, this does not robustly contradict a phosphorylation-driven NF-κB coupling; no supportive mRNA pattern for pathway activation observed
  • Confidence: medium (good disease-relevant cell-type coverage; mechanism is post-translational so transcriptomics are indirect).
