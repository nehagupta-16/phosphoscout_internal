Overview

What the variant is and why it's being analyzed

FieldValue
GeneBRAF
Mutation (CDS)
Mutation (AA)p.P367S

BRAF p.P367S (PRO367SER) lies outside the catalytic kinase domain (UniProt kinase domain ~457–717) and has been reported in melanoma with functional annotations suggesting activating/likely oncogenic behavior in curated knowledgebases and increased transformation in Ba/F3 assays [1, 5, 8]. Within the mutation-centered 29-mer, EPSD-supported phosphosites are present at S363, S364, S365, and T373, and the P367S substitution introduces a new central Ser at position 367; these sites provide local phosphorylation context for potential regulatory effects [9, 10]. Knowledgebase entries also note detection of BRAF P367S in clinical contexts (e.g., melanoma) and classify it as likely oncogenic [5].

Hypotheses

Mechanistic explanations generated from the overview and literature.

BRAF p.P367S creates a new AMPK/MARK-family phospho-motif at S367 that enables energy-stress–tuned rewiring of RAF–MEK–ERK output in melanoma

BRAF p.P367S is reported as likely oncogenic/activating in melanoma-relevant functional annotation resources [5, 8]. The P→S substitution creates a new, solvent-accessible phospho-acceptor at BRAF S367 (center_pos=367) with predicted kinase-substrate specificity gains for PRKAA1/PRKAA2 (AMPKα1/α2) and multiple AMPK-related kinases (e.g., MARK1-4, SIK1-3, NIM1K) (direction=gain), implying that energy-sensing kinase programs could newly target this BRAF N-terminal region in the mutant [9, 10]. Because AMPK can directly phosphorylate BRAF to attenuate MEK–ERK signaling output (demonstrated at a different BRAF site, S729) and oncogenic BRAF signaling intersects with the LKB1–AMPK axis in melanoma, the mutant-created S367 motif could act as a new node for stress-dependent modulation of MAPK signaling amplitude and/or pathway engagement, thereby supporting tumor cell fitness under metabolic stress (prediction; requires phospho readouts to establish directionality at S367) [3, 8].

Predicted observations

  • Targeted phosphoproteomics (PRM/SRM) detects a mutant-specific phosphopeptide encompassing BRAF pS367 in P367S-expressing cells, and its abundance changes upon AMPK activation (e.g., AICAR/metformin/glucose starvation) versus AMPK inhibition/knockdown (direction to be empirically determined) [11].
  • In isogenic cells, AMPK perturbation (PRKAA1/2 knockdown or pharmacologic inhibition) produces a larger shift in MEK/ERK pathway markers (e.g., pMEK, pERK) in BRAF P367S than in BRAF WT, consistent with a new AMPK-coupled regulatory input in the mutant (discriminating dependency prediction) [3, 11].
  • A BRAF S367A reversion (in the P367S background) reduces AMPK-dependent modulation of ERK output and alters proliferation/survival specifically under metabolic stress conditions compared with P367S, consistent with S367 acting as the causal node (direction to be empirically determined) [11].
Laboratory validation

Suggested models

  • Isogenic melanoma cell model engineered to express BRAF WT vs BRAF P367S at comparable levels (e.g., CRISPR knock-in or inducible cDNA) [3, 5].
  • Ba/F3 or other transformation-amenable line (as used in functional annotation contexts) for orthogonal phenotype assays [8].

Assays

  • Site-specific phospho readout: targeted LC–MS/MS for BRAF pS367 peptide (and adjacent site peptides as controls) across AMPK-activating vs inhibiting conditions [11].
  • Kinase-perturbation test: PRKAA1/PRKAA2 CRISPR/siRNA or AMPK inhibitors/activators, with downstream pMEK/pERK quantification and growth/colony assays under metabolic stress vs nutrient-replete conditions [3, 11].
  • Causality/epistasis: compare BRAF P367S vs P367S+S367A (phospho-null) vs P367S+S367D/E (phosphomimetic proxy) for (i) presence/absence of pS367 signal and (ii) AMPK-perturbation sensitivity of ERK output and stress-survival phenotypes; rescue logic is that re-introducing an S367 phospho-mimic should restore the AMPK-dependent phenotype even when S367A abolishes it (acknowledging Asp/Glu are imperfect phospho-mimetics) [11].

Controls and confounders

  • Control for global AMPK activity using canonical AMPK substrate readouts (e.g., pACC) to verify effective AMPK modulation without assuming BRAF pS367 directionality a priori [11].
  • Control for BRAF expression level and subcellular localization across constructs to avoid indirect effects on MAPK output [1, 3].
  • Include a BRAF V600E context as a comparator for MAPK-addicted signaling behaviors, without conflating mechanisms at distinct sites [3].

Feasibility notes

The key feasibility risk is generating a robust pS367 assay; targeted phosphoproteomics is the most direct option given the novelty of the site and lack of established antibodies [11].

Limitations
  • Kinase events represent predicted specificity rewiring, not measured phosphorylation; pS367 existence and directionality under AMPK modulation must be experimentally established [9, 10].
  • AMPK is supported here as a plausible kinase based on predicted PRKAA1/2 specificity gain at S367 and known AMPK→BRAF phosphorylation at another site; direct biochemical validation for S367 is required [11].
  • The mechanistic link to melanoma fitness is inferred from BRAF/MAPK centrality and the oncogenic classification of P367S; phenotypic consequences may be cell-state dependent [3, 5].

PTM-level gain of AMPK/MARK-family motif on BRAF S367; mRNA shifts are not required, but higher BRAF and AMPK/MARK expression in melanoma could be consistent with increased capacity for S367 phosphorylation; SIKs may or may not change

Alignment assessment

Multiple AMPK/MARK-family members and BRAF show coordinated modest increases with borderline q≈0.10, consistent with greater kinase capacity in melanoma; SIKs largely unchanged; however, evidence is indirect (PTM mechanism) and comparison is cross-cell-type, which can confound cancer vs normal differences

Conclusion

Overall verdict: SUPPORTS (low confidence) — modest, coordinated upregulation of BRAF and several AMPK/MARK genes in melanoma is directionally consistent with the proposed AMPK/MARK-tuned BRAF signaling, but PTM-specific and cross-cell-type limitations temper confidence.
