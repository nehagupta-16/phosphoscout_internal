Overview

What the variant is and why it's being analyzed

Mutation (AA)p.T28S

PLAG1 p.T28S substitutes a threonine with serine at residue 28. Position 28 (T) in human PLAG1 is reported as an experimentally supported phosphosite in EPSD, indicating prior phosphorylation evidence at this site [5, 6]. PLAG1 itself is a proto-oncogenic transcription factor frequently activated by 8q12 translocations in pleomorphic adenoma and implicated in IGF2 upregulation [1, 2, 3]. Within the mutation-centered 29-mer, we detected an evidence-supported phosphosite 15-mer window centered on residue 28 that passed solvent-accessibility criteria (within-class S/T change enables continued Ser/Thr kinase targeting).

Hypotheses

Mechanistic explanations generated from the overview and literature.

PLAG1 p.T28S rewires PIM1 kinase specificity at the N-terminal phosphosite (center_pos 28), shifting PLAG1-driven oncogenic transcription toward IGF2/PI3K–AKT programs in PLAG1-driven salivary pleomorphic adenoma

PLAG1 p.T28S alters the known N-terminal phosphosite at residue 28 (Thr→Ser; center_pos 28) and is predicted to increase kinase–substrate specificity for PIM1 (gain; Δ=0.256) and DYRK3 (gain; Δ=0.320) at the 15-mer window GKRKRGESKPRKNFP, relative to wildtype GKRKRGETKPRKNFP [5, 6]. Because PLAG1 is an oncogenic transcription factor whose tumorigenic output is frequently linked to IGF2 upregulation in PLAG1-driven pleomorphic adenoma, a plausible consequence is that this specificity rewiring changes PLAG1 regulatory state (to be tested by site phospho-occupancy) and biases its transcriptional program toward stronger IGF2-mediated mitogenic signaling (IGF2→IGF1R→PI3K–AKT) in PLAG1-activated salivary tumors [1, 2, 3]. This hypothesis is mechanistically testable by determining whether PIM1 dependence emerges for phosphorylation at Ser28 and whether Ser28 phosphorylation state causally tunes PLAG1 target-gene activation and downstream PI3K–AKT pathway markers in relevant models [7].

Disease context

  • Pleomorphic adenoma of the salivary gland (PLAG1-rearranged/activated) [1, 3, 4]
  • Other PLAG1-activated tumors with IGF2 involvement (e.g., lipoblastoma, hepatoblastoma) (inference from PLAG1 oncogene versatility) [7]
